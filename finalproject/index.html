Figure Eight

The Figure Eight Knot is one of the most widely used knots in sailing, providing a secure and easy-to-untie option.

Clove Hitch

Used for securing a rope to a post, the Clove Hitch Knot is a simple and versatile knot often used by sailors.

Bowline

The Bowline is often referred to as the "king of knots," creating a secure loop that doesn't slip under load.

Square

The Square Knot is used for joining two ropes of the same diameter. It is one of the easiest knots to tie and untie.

Taut Line

The Taut-Line Hitch is used for securing a rope to a post or tree and allows for adjusting the tension easily.

Sheet Bend

The Sheet Bend is ideal for joining two ropes of unequal thickness, especially when one rope is heavier than the other.

Rolling Hitch

The Rolling Hitch is commonly used for tying a rope to a pole or spar, providing excellent holding power.

Double Fisherman

The Double Fisherman's Knot is used for joining two ropes or cords, often used in climbing or rescue situations.
